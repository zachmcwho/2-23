Temples


Why are there temples?

The Lord said, “Build a house to my name, for the Most High to dwell therein” (D&C 124:27). The Lord’s Spirit dwells in His temples. The temple is a house of God. It is a place where we make covenants (or promises) with Heavenly Father. If we keep our covenants, we will be able to live with Him again.

Baptism and Confirmation Are Essential

The fourth Article of Faith states that the first ordinances of the gospel of Jesus Christ are “baptism by immersion for the remission of sins” and the “laying on of hands for the gift of the Holy Ghost.” Jesus taught that baptism and confirmation are needed for all who want to follow Him and return to our Heavenly Father after this life.

God Loves All of His Children

Every person is a child of God. All are precious to Him. He knows them and He loves them. He declared, “For behold, this is my work and my glory—to bring to pass the immortality and eternal life of man” (Moses 1:39). He has provided a way for all of His children to return to Him after this life. That way is the gospel of Jesus Christ
